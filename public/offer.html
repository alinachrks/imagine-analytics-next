History about Aluminium, and some facts.

History

Aluminum is a chemical element. It is a soft, non-magnetic and ductile material in boron group. The chief ore of aluminum is Bauxite. Aluminum is a silvery white metal with a 13th element in the periodic table. Its atomic number is 13 and 26.98153 is its atomic mass and it is popularly known as Al.

What aluminium consists of?

Aluminum is the utmost widespread metal on the world, making up 10% of the Earth's core mass. After oxygen and silicon, Aluminum is the most common chemical in our planet. It has many applications but pure aluminum does not exist because it can bend easily with many other elements.

What materials were used?

An alloy is commonly known as the mixture of two or more metals made by melting and mixing the selected metals. Thus a mixture obtained from mixing has some different and advancement in properties from individual metals. Aluminum alloys are which aluminum (Al) is employed as a major metal. Copper, magnesium, manganese, silicon, tin and zinc are the most commonly using typical alloying elements.
